>_ my minecraft world :3

I have been playing Minecraft since 2011. No other game has had such a profound impact in my life. I love playing it both alone and with friends.

Interestingly, though, despite having played for so long, I had yet to commit to a single world for more than a few months. This didn't bother me, though. It kept the game feeling fresh and made it easy to experience new updates' content; this was best exemplified by a series of yearly SMPs from 2017 to 2023. Many of my warmest memories in the game come from these SMPs; at its peak (Seasons 4-5) we had around 15 active members, most of which were my closest friends in real life. There was beauty in starting over each time, but eventually we tired of it.

Season 7 (2023) would be the last SMP. Given the lack of motivation to play survival, and having noticed that the additions of the 1.20 update seemed to cater more towards building, we decided to restart the world, keeping the same seed but switching the server's gamemode to creative, which we hadn't done in many years. Thus, the CMP was born, to great and immediate success. In a couple months we'd created an agglomeration of builds larger than anything we'd ever done: Grug City.

The server has closed and re-opened several times since June 2023, primarily during summer/winter breaks, when we're all free and on the same time zone. During off-seasons, I've continued to play on the world very regularly, since I own the world save. I've developed quite an attachment to this world; it's become somewhat of a "third place" for me, a place where I log in to relax and unwind, no matter where I may be in real life. I have no plans on quitting.

>_ coding & features

There's no point in re-inventing the wheel, especially in programming. Here are some tools, libraries, and tutorials that greatly helped me in the creation of this website and its features.

  • 98.css
    • CSS library to easily recreate windows-98-styled windows!
  • Aleclownes
    • Used for the CRT screen door effect in the center sections and the color separation effect in the main header.
  • Catbox
    • Image and file hosting service.
    • I used this to store the music player songs, as well as really large files like the home page background GIF.
  • CSS-Tricks
    • "A website about making websites."
    • This is where I got the Typewriter animation and the old-timey terminal window styling.
  • Ezgif
    • Online GIF editor. Lots of useful tools like cropping, flipping, compressing, and converting to/from MP4.
    • Used for Ame-Chan's icon in the music player and the Minecraft skin gif.
  • FC2 Counter
    • Visitor counters compatible with statically-hosted websites.
    • I used both the total visitors as well as the concurrent visitors.
  • HTML, CSS, JavaScript
    • Pretty self explanatory.
  • JQuery
    • Streamlines JS event handling. For websites like this, I find it cleaner than vanilla JS.
  • LoveBerry
    • Template used for the Music Player, which I modified to fit my site's structure.
    • This website also has other useful code snippets and is super-cool overall!!
  • Tamanotchi
    • Free and easily embeddable Tamagotchi virtual pets.
  • VSCode + LiveServer
    • My text editor of choice. Open-source and cross-platform. Does the job.
    • LiveServer is a VSCode extension that allows you to host a local server and see your changes in real-time. Not essential but saves time.
  • W3Schools
    • Tons of useful tutorials for learning or refreshing your knowledge of HTML/CSS/JS.
